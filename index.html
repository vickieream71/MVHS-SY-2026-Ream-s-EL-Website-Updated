Course Descriptions

What students do in each class.

Intensive ELL English 2

This course builds upon a foundational knowledge of English to improve students' ability to communicate for social and academic purposes. Students will enhance their vocabulary, master basic and progressive grammar tenses, and develop the skills to read and interpret simple texts, instructions, and forms. The curriculum emphasizes a supportive learning environment with a heavy focus on oral proficiency and text-level skills. Key activities include structured group discussions, short presentations, guided paragraph writing, and reading level-appropriate, high-interest literature such as short stories, myths, and non-fiction. The ultimate goal is to equip students with the necessary communication skills to transition into higher-level ELA courses and succeed in the broader school environment.

ELL English 1

This course is designed for English Language Learners who are in the expanding phase of language acquisition and are able to communicate in English but need to acquire the technical academic language required for grade-level content. Students in this course will focus on developing critical reading, writing, listening, and speaking skills within various academic and social contexts, using both high-interest modified and abridged authentic resources.

ELL Resource

This performance-based course is a support class designed to foster English language acquisition and provide targeted academic assistance to high school English Language Learners (ELLs) at various proficiency levels. Instruction is tailored to students' individual language needs, as determined by language proficiency assessments (e.g., WIDA ELD Standards). The curriculum focuses on developing the critical listening, speaking, reading, and writing skills essential for daily communication and academic success.
